House Rules

General Rules

Flanking

Flanking and its associated optional rules are used: Flanking, pinning, and height advantage

  • Flanking. Allied creatures have advantage on attack rolls with melee weapon attacks when on opposite sides of a creature no larger than one size class larger than the smallest of the two allied creatures. Creatures are on opposite sides if both creatures are within 5 feet of opposing corners of their target.
  • Pinning. A creature has advantage on melee weapon attacks if their target creature is the same or smaller size and directly between the attacking creature and a wall.
  • Height. A creature has advantage on ranged weapon attacks if they are at least 10 feet above and 5 feet away from the target creature.

Advantage

Multiple sources of advantage add a d4 to the roll. Multiple sources of disadvantage subtract a d4 from the roll. This does not stack; no bonus or penalty will be greater than 1d4.

Inspiration

Inspiration can be used to reroll any roll for a character you can see or gain gp equal to the character's levelX10. A player can't transfer inspiration between their characters.

Player Summons

Multiple player summons will use mob combat rules and share their turn with the summoner.

Massive Damage

When a character takes damage equal to or greater than its hit point maximum from a single source, they die immediately. Critical hits will not inflict lasting injuries.

Unconscious Roleplay

For roleplay purposes, when you hit 0 hp and start rolling death saves, you may choose whether or not you are unconscious. You are unable to take any actions besides speaking as a free action if you remain awake.

Martial Skill

Martial checks adeptly assess other characters and enemies. You can roll a d20 to attempt to rate a creature's strength relative to you or your party. On a successful martial check, each creature with traits will have its number of traits and one trait with its effects revealed at random.
Fighters, Rangers, Hunters, Bastions, and Paladins may gain proficiency in this skill at level 1.

Options

Contributing Encouters

Any player may propose a pair of random encounters via DM to your DM: one negative, one positive. The DM reserves the right to deny entries.

Custom Spells

If the DM changes a spell's effects or balance after a player has taken it, the player may replace it with another spell of the same or lower level.

Brutality Mode

Death save failures can only be removed on long rests with food and drink, similar to levels of exhaustion. Rolling a natural 20 will not give a character 1 hp unless the player already has at least one successful death save.
