Easifa

The Eldest and Humblest Kin

The True Mother, goddess of origination, Solis, did very little to affect the mortal world directly, save for the creation of the easifan race. She fostered them to inhabit the then-barren world, with cooperative instincts to overcome any hardship. This care, in turn, caused Easifa to be eternally grateful. They were the first mortal species, and their civilization observed as gods shaped and molded the world around them into a veritable paradise.

Despite the longevity of their society, Easifa are content with each other, granting little concern to technological advancement. They instead focus on oral tradition and shared stories, as the creators of the language now known as Common. While they have little in the way of written history, the stories they share are rich, vivid windows into the past.

Strongest of Bonds

The Easifa are hard-wired to work well with others; it is necessary for their survival. They take great joy in learning new points of view. Merchants will travel through the harshest conditions across the world and share wonderful stories with those they meet along the way.

Playable Traits

Ability Score Increase. Your Dexterity increases by 2, and your Wisdom increases by 1.

Age. Easifa mature at the same rate as humans, reaching maturity at age 20. On average they live about 100 years, but few die of natural causes.

Size. Easifan males stand between 4 and 5 feet, while females can stand as tall as 6. Both weigh 130 pounds on average. Your size is Medium.

Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.

Languages. You can speak, read, and write Common and two other languages of your choice.

Desert Fury. Once per turn, you can spend a hit die to gain an additional bonus action on your turn. If you have used this trait more than twice since your last short rest, gain a level of exhaustion at the end of your turn.

Easifan Greed. Whenever you make an Intelligence check to appraise an item, add double your proficiency bonus to the roll instead of your normal proficiency bonus.

Taught by the Sand. You ignore difficult terrain made of sand, snow, or rock.
